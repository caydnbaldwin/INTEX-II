# Professional Nonprofit Website Features — Recommendations for Lunas

Based on research of charitywater.org, thewaterproject.org, rescue.org, savethechildren.org, worldvision.org, and ijm.org.

---

## Tier 1: High Impact, Easy to Add

### 1. Trust Badges & Footer Upgrade
- Add Charity Navigator, Candid (GuideStar), BBB, Charity Watch seals to footer
- Display EIN number alongside 501(c)(3) status
- Link to annual report PDF
- Add "How your dollar is spent" pie chart (e.g. 87% programs / 7% admin / 6% fundraising)

### 2. Social Media Links
- Add Instagram, Facebook, Twitter/X, LinkedIn, YouTube icons in footer
- Every top nonprofit has these prominently in the footer

### 3. Newsletter Signup
- Email subscription form in footer or as a section on the homepage
- "Stay updated on the lives you're changing"
- Even a simple form that collects emails is better than nothing

### 4. Cost-Per-Beneficiary Messaging
- Reframe donation tiers: "$30/month = one month of safe shelter", "$50 = school supplies for a year"
- Charity:water and World Vision both do this — makes donations feel concrete

### 5. Named Leadership / Team Page
- "Meet Our Team" page with founder, executive director, 3-5 key staff
- Photos, names, brief bios
- Builds personal trust — donors want to know who's behind the organization

---

## Tier 2: Medium Effort, Strong Impact

### 6. About Us / Our Story Page
- Organization history, mission, vision, values
- Theory of change: how does Lunas actually work?
- "Why the Philippines" section with context on the problem
- Partnership model (local government, NGOs, schools)

### 7. How It Works / Our Model Page
- Visual journey: "Child is identified → Intake → Safehouse placement → Education → Counseling → Reintegration"
- Explain the safehouse model, what daily life looks like
- Address common questions about the approach

### 8. Monthly Recurring Giving Pathway
- "Become a Monthly Sustainer" option alongside one-time donations
- Frame as community: "Join 200+ monthly supporters"
- Charity:water's "The Spring" program is the gold standard here

### 9. Gift Catalog / Tangible Giving Options
- "Provide one month of safe housing" ($100)
- "Fund vocational training for one youth" ($250)
- "Sponsor a girl's full year of education" ($500)
- World Vision's gift catalog drives massive engagement

### 10. Verified Outcomes Data
- Show: % of residents who complete education, employment rates post-discharge, family reunion rates
- Compare to benchmarks if available
- Most child nonprofits don't show this — it's a differentiator

## Tier 3: Nice Polish

### 11. FAQ Page
- "How do you protect residents' privacy and safety?"
- "Where does my money go?"
- "What happens to residents after they leave?"
- "How are safehouses chosen and operated?"
- Proactively answers donor concerns

### 12. Blog / News Section
- Monthly impact updates, staff spotlights, success stories
- SEO benefit + demonstrates the organization is active
- Can reuse resident story content

### 13. Impact Pathway Visualization
- Animated journey: "Enter safehouse → Month 1: Education → Month 6: Job training → Graduation"
- Show multiple pathways (reunification, independent living, education)
- Interactive, scrollable timeline of a resident's typical journey

### 14. Real-Time Impact Counter
- "Meals provided this month: 2,847" / "Kids in school: 156"
- Creates urgency and shows scale
- Can be static numbers that update periodically

### 15. Testimonials Section
- Quotes from donors, partners, community leaders
- "I've seen firsthand the transformation..." with name and photo
- Social proof drives giving

### 16. Contact Form
- Web form for inquiries (not just an email address)
- Separate options: "I want to donate", "I want to volunteer", "Media inquiry", "General question"

### 17. Terms of Service
- Separate from Privacy Policy
- Standard nonprofit ToS

### 18. Accessibility Statement
- WCAG compliance commitment
- Contact info for accessibility concerns

### 19. Custom 404 Page
- Branded error page with helpful links instead of redirect to home
- "This page doesn't exist, but these girls do — see how you can help"

### 20. Multi-Language Support
- English + Tagalog at minimum (for Philippine context)
- Signals local commitment

- Interactive map (matches charity:water)
